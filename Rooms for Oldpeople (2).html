Simple and Neutral:

To live comfortably, leisure at late afternoon is the common wish of man. After thirty years with all worries, everyone desires to own an ideal place to settle the burden of living happily.This bedroom provides them with all of comfort.

Basic but Classic:

For most of us we spend our lives seeing our bedrooms as a safe sanctuary – a place to relax, unwind and sleep. But, getting older or living with dementia can make the mundane much more challenging and turn those things we take for granted quite dangerous.This bedroom suits best for them.

Slip-Resistant:

One of the biggest risks while caring for the elderly is falls. This room replaces your smooth tiles for anti-skid flooring. The texture or friction offers grip and ensures your elderly parents can move without the fear of accidents..

Natural Lighting:

Of course with age, your parent’s eyes are going to fail them. This room makes the best use of natural lighting by incorporating more windows. Also incorporates bedside lamps with switches that are within their reach, should they need something in the night. Keeping the room airy and spacious along with generous lighting will ensure less strain for your elderly parent’s vision..

Easy on hands:

Ease of use should be on top of your mind when installing hardware – replace doorknobs with lever style handles, use large drawer knobs and open shutters wherever possible. Metal handles can usually be painful for elderly parents. The hardware has to function smoothly with minimal effort..

Safe Walkways:

This room avoid cramping your aging parent’s room with a lot of furniture. Instead, invest in minimal pieces to hold their essentials like a bedside table, a chest of drawers and a reading chair – depending on their habits.
